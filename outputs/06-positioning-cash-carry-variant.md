## The Dunford Canvas: Cash & Carry Variant

### Step 1: Competitive Alternatives

What does a C&C DSO use today when Wayroo doesn't exist?

| Alternative | What It Does | Why It Fails C&C DSOs |
|-------------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Square** | POS and card processing at events | One channel only (in-person). No inventory, no customer record to corporate, no branded invoice, no online storefront. |
| **Shopify** | Ecommerce storefront | One channel only (online). No POS, no invoicing with payment links, no inventory management for reps, no corporate visibility. |
| **Venmo / PayPal** | Informal payments and invoicing | One channel only (payments/invoicing). No record visible to corporate, no brand control, no inventory. |
| **Excel / Google Sheets** | Rep inventory tracking | Not a sales channel at all — manual tracking only, abandoned after 2 events, never connected to any transaction. |
| **Shopify VO (corporate-managed)** | Corporate storefront and rep dashboard | Corporate-side only — no rep personal inventory management, no POS, no invoicing channel. |
| **Nothing** | Corporate ignores the C&C channel entirely | The default; each rep uses a different tool; compliance risk accumulates silently. |

**The honest composite:** A C&C rep today uses Square to take payment, Venmo or cash when Square isn't available, a mental model or spreadsheet for inventory, and her phone notes for customer names. The DSO sees none of it.

### Step 4: Target Customers

Who values this most, right now:

**Primary buyer:** VP Field Operations / Director of Distributor Relations at a Cash & Carry DSO  
- Feels personal accountability for rep experience and brand integrity  
- Has already identified that reps need better tools — doesn't know what exists  
- Will champion internally once they see the rep experience demo  

**Secondary decision maker:** CTO / VP Technology at the same DSO  
- Controls integration decisions — Shopify, SSO, payment separation  
- Will block the deal if the integration story isn't technically credible  
- Can be won with documentation and a dedicated technical scoping call  

**Segment filters (qualify early):**  
- >30% of reps carry and sell personal inventory (cash-and-carry model confirmed)  
- Reps actively participate in vendor events, markets, pop-ups  
- DSO has zero corporate visibility into rep-held inventory after wholesale ships  
- Reps use Square, Venmo, PayPal, or cash for in-person transactions  

**What disqualifies a C&C DSO:**  
- >90% of rep activity is direct-to-customer catalog fulfillment (no in-person inventory selling)  
- DSO already has real-time field inventory visibility through another tool  
- DSO has no interest in rep customer data (rare; mostly a budget/priority signal)

### Step 5: Market Category

**How to frame the category:**  
Do not pitch Wayroo as a POS tool, an inventory tool, or a storefront tool. Each of those frames puts Wayroo in competition with a single-use tool (Square, Shopify, Excel) and forces a feature-by-feature fight.

**Winning frame:** Wayroo is the **rep business operating system** — the single platform that connects every part of a cash-and-carry rep's in-person selling activity back to corporate, and that turns the rep into a real sub-business owner under the DSO's umbrella.

For C&C DSOs specifically, the frame tightens further: Wayroo is **the infrastructure that makes the cash-and-carry channel visible, compliant, and valuable** — instead of invisible, untracked, and a liability.

### Step 6: Relevant Trends

Why this matters *now* (not in two years):

1. **FTC enforcement posture:** The FTC has increased scrutiny of direct selling organizations that cannot demonstrate products reach real end customers. Every untracked cash/Venmo sale is accumulating liability. This is not a future risk; it's active. The retail sales documentation requirement is enforceable now.

2. **Party Platform Solutions price hikes + Thatcher bankruptcy:** C&C DSOs that relied on Party Platform Solutions or Thatcher for back-office are actively evaluating alternatives. Their migration window is open. Wayroo is a natural landing spot.

3. **Aice raising $15M for AI features:** The rep-facing tool market is heating up. DSOs that don't lock in an infrastructure choice in 2026 may end up on a platform that has already built switching costs around its rep base.

4. **Ex-LuLaRoe rep populations are aging in:** The LuLaRoe collapse (2017–2020) seeded a generation of ex-LuLaRoe reps across jewelry, nail, and beauty DSOs — including Color Street. These reps already know the Wayroo lineage. The longer DSOs wait, the more this cohort turns over.

## Messaging Pillars for Sales and Marketing Use

Four pillars, ordered by opening power for a cold C&C DSO outreach or a first discovery call:

---

### Pillar 1: Every Untracked Sale Is Three Problems at Once

**Use:** Opening move in outreach, first pain hook in discovery  
**Headline:** "Venmo doesn't satisfy the FTC. PayPal won't help at tax time. And neither gives you back the customer."

The three mandates every untracked cash or Venmo transaction violates simultaneously:
- **FTC retail sales documentation** — the rep selling into thin air (no end customer record) is exactly the inventory loading pattern the FTC cracks down on
- **IRS / state tax compliance** — rep 1099-NEC and sales tax jurisdiction tracking require records that Venmo and cash do not produce
- **Corporate marketing access** — the customer identity disappears with the Venmo transaction; corporate marketing can never reach that buyer

**Discovery question:** "How do you currently document rep earnings for 1099 issuance when they sell at events or through Venmo?"  
**Objection it handles:** "Our reps use PayPal and it's fine" → Three separate failure points, not one preference. All three are solved by the same Wayroo feature.

---

### Pillar 2: Your Reps' Customers Are Your Customers Too — You Just Can't See Them

**Use:** Revenue upsell angle after compliance hook; the "gain" framing that softens the regulatory pressure  
**Headline:** "How many unique customers did your stylists sell to last quarter? Wayroo tells you. Venmo doesn't."

- Every rep customer record Wayroo captures is pushed to corporate CRM
- Product launches, loyalty programs, retargeting, win-back flows — all to buyers who have already demonstrated willingness to purchase
- For a DSO with 20,000 reps each with a handful of repeat buyers: the size of the invisible customer base is staggering

**Discovery question:** "Do you know how many unique customers your reps sold to last quarter?"  
**Objection it handles:** "We market through our reps" → The rep distributes; the customer relationship belongs to the DSO. Every untracked sale is a customer the marketing team can never reach independently.

---

### Pillar 3: See Your Field Inventory for the First Time

**Use:** The field operations angle; appeals directly to the Field Champion persona  
**Headline:** "Know what every rep owns — before a product expires, before a customer complains, before a style sells out."

- Rep orders wholesale from within Wayroo → inventory appears immediately on order → rep marks received as stock arrives → DSO sees confirmed rep holdings in real time
- Throwback campaigns targeted to reps who actually hold the SKU
- Expiration tracking at the rep level — corporate intervenes before the product is sold to a customer out of condition
- Brand catalog control via replicated site — the DSO's official product images and descriptions populate every rep's replicated site automatically; reps cannot override this. Structural brand enforcement, not a policy.

**Discovery question:** "How do you currently run throwback or liquidation campaigns when you know some reps are sitting on old inventory?"  
**Objection it handles:** "We ship it and it's their inventory now" → It's their inventory but your brand, your compliance record, and your missed demand signal.

---

### Pillar 4: Built for Reps Who've Done This Before

**Use:** Rep adoption risk objection; LuLaRoe reference closer  
**Headline:** "Built by the team behind the LuLaRoe app. Your stylists already know it."

- LuLaRoe was the largest cash-and-carry deployment in direct selling history; Wayroo built the rep app
- Many C&C rep populations (especially nail, beauty, jewelry) include ex-LuLaRoe stylists — they've already used this workflow
- Paparazzi: 4,000 MAUs on a corporate-mandate rollout — proof that mandate-driven adoption works
- Rep experience: barcode POS, "My Stash" storefront, automated inventory, Spendback

**Discovery question:** "What percentage of your top stylists came from another DSO? Do you know how many are ex-LuLaRoe?"  
**Objection it handles:** "Rep adoption is always the problem with new tools" → Paparazzi mandate proof + LuLaRoe credibility + the app was built for exactly this model.

### Pillar 5: Selling Time vs. Admin Time (Cross-Account Validated — 4 Accounts)

**Use:** Universal opener for any rep-focused conversation; strongest ROI framing for the Field Champion persona  
**Headline:** "The time your reps spend on admin is time they're not selling. Wayroo makes admin a byproduct of selling — not a separate job."

- **Cross-account validation:** Confirmed across 3 current ByDesign clients and Color Street (as of May 2026). Brick B (Color Street): *"Reps spend lots of time doing admin tasks such as printing shipping labels, maintaining inventory spreadsheets, and none of these activities generate revenue."*
- **The mechanism:** Bluetooth scanning at vendor events auto-decrements inventory on each sale. In-app wholesale ordering means SKUs are already loaded when inventory arrives. Invoicing is generated automatically on each transaction. Shipping labels print from within the app. The rep's entire administrative surface collapses into their normal selling flow — nothing extra to do, no separate tool to log into.
- **The ROI frame:** If a rep saves 3 hours per week of non-revenue admin and converts even half of that into selling time, the revenue impact is direct and measurable. This is the metric to build into the Paparazzi case study.

**Discovery question:** "How much time do your reps spend per week on admin tasks — spreadsheets, shipping, inventory counts — and how much of that generates any revenue?"  
**Objection it handles:** "Our reps are used to Square" → Square takes payment. It doesn't track inventory, print shipping labels, or manage wholesale orders. Every task the rep does outside of Square is still manual — Wayroo eliminates those tasks entirely.  
**Why this is pillar 5:** Four-account validation makes this the most broadly resonant rep-level proof point in the portfolio. It works for C&C and standard DSO. It works for the Field Champion (rep experience) and the CEO (rep retention). Lead with it when you need a fast emotional land.

## Positioning Usage Guide

### When to use the C&C variant vs. the standard DSO positioning

| Signal | Use |
|--------|-----|
| >30% of reps carry personal inventory | C&C variant — lead with Pillar 1 (compliance), close with Pillar 4 (adoption) |
| DSO mentions vendor events, markets, or pop-ups | C&C variant |
| DSO mentions Square as primary rep payment tool | C&C variant — Square is the incumbent competitor |
| DSO is on standard back-office (replicated site, catalog orders only) | Standard DSO positioning |
| DSO has not mentioned rep physical inventory | Qualify first before using C&C framing |

### Opening line for C&C outreach (email or cold call)

> "Quick question — for a DSO your size, what percentage of your reps sell from personal inventory vs. fulfilling catalog orders? I ask because we just worked through a C&C-specific scenario with [reference DSO] and the compliance and inventory visibility story is pretty different from the standard Wayroo pitch."

This line does three things: qualifies the segment, signals research, and creates curiosity without a feature dump.

### Integration story (for Edmond archetype — lead this in technical conversations)

Do not open with features. Open with: "Let's start with your current stack — what does the rep log into today, and what does that pull from?"  
Then map Wayroo's integration points to whatever they describe — don't assume Shopify until they confirm it.  
Then send a technical scoping document before the follow-up call. Do not leave a technical conversation without a written follow-up.

## What This Positioning Is NOT

- This is not the standard Wayroo positioning (rep activation, one-stop-shop for reps). Those messages work for standard DSO ICP. For C&C, lead with compliance and inventory visibility.
- This is not Track 2 (direct-to-rep) messaging. Everything here targets the DSO corporate buyer — the VP Field Ops and the CTO. Rep-facing messaging for Track 2 does not yet exist and should not be extrapolated from this document.
- This is not finalized for paid campaigns. One data point validates the segment; three to five validate the positioning. Test these pillars in discovery calls and note which one generates the strongest reaction. Update this file after each C&C discovery call.
