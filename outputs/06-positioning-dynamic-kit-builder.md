# Dynamic Kit Builder — Positioning Statement

**Date:** April 30, 2026  
**Feature:** Dynamic Kit Builder (Freedom Back Office)  
**Framework:** April Dunford (adapted)

---

## Competitive Alternatives

What DSOs use today instead of Dynamic Kit Builder:

1. **Shopify + third-party bundle apps** (Bundler, FastBundle, etc.) — most common "modern" alternative; carries transaction fees, integration overhead, and split data
2. **Static/fixed bundles** — hardcoded by dev team in existing back office; no shopper control, no conditional logic
3. **Manual kit ordering** — phone, email, or spreadsheet; no scale, no conversion optimization
4. **No kits at all** — individual product sales only; DSO leaves average order value on the table
5. **Custom dev work** — one-off configurator builds; expensive, fragile, not maintained
6. **Exigo native bundling** — rigid, not shopper-facing, no guided experience

---

## Unique Attributes

What Dynamic Kit Builder has that none of the alternatives do:

| Attribute | Why It's Unique |
|-----------|----------------|
| Native to Freedom | No Shopify account, no integration, no extra transaction fees — already inside the platform |
| Conditional step logic | Trigger specific builder steps based on prior selections — Shopify bundle apps are static |
| Compensation engine integration | Kit sales calculate into comp plans automatically — no manual reconciliation |
| Dual-audience (rep + shopper) | One system serves both field reps and end customers |
| Built-in promotions engine | Run targeted offers inside the kit builder — no separate promotion tool needed |
| Unlimited properties & attributes | Handles complex MLM configs: enrollment packs, seasonal collections, multi-variant bundles |

## Target Customers (Who Cares Most)

**Primary:**
- DSOs currently on Shopify or evaluating it — frustrated by transaction fees and integration overhead
- Party plan DSOs — kit/bundle selling is their primary sales model; Party Platform Solutions and Thatcher clients in active displacement
- DSOs with complex enrollment or starter kit programs where static bundles break down

**Secondary:**
- Existing Freedom clients being pitched Wayroo or Shopify by reps who want a better product UX

## Market Category

**Not:** "A kit builder" or "a product configurator"  
**Yes:** Native guided commerce for direct selling — the modern ecommerce experience DSOs went to Shopify for, built into the platform that already runs your comp plan, genealogy, and promotions.

## Relevant Trends

1. **Shopify has set the UX standard.** DSOs are judged against Shopify by their reps and prospects. "Your shopping cart looks outdated" is now a top sales objection.
2. **Party plan sector in displacement.** Party Platform Solutions raised prices; Thatcher is going bankrupt. Their clients need a kit-capable platform — now.
3. **Platform consolidation.** DSOs are moving away from fragmented stacks. A kit builder that lives inside your back office is the consolidation argument.
4. **Average order value pressure.** Kit/bundle selling is how DSOs grow revenue per transaction without adding headcount or running constant promotions.

---

## Positioning Statement

> For direct selling organizations who need a modern, guided shopping experience but can't afford Shopify's transaction fees or integration overhead, **Dynamic Kit Builder** is a native guided commerce tool built into Freedom that lets reps and customers build custom kits — with conditional logic, built-in promotions, and automatic comp plan integration.
>
> Unlike Shopify bundle apps or static back-office configurations, Dynamic Kit Builder is already inside your Freedom platform — every kit sale flows directly into your compensation engine, promotions, and inventory with no sync, no fees, and no third-party dependency.
>
> This matters now because DSOs are losing prospects to the "Shopify looks better" objection, and party plan clients need a flexible kit experience that their current platforms can no longer deliver.
