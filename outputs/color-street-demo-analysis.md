# Color Street + Wayroo Demo Analysis
**Date:** May 1, 2026  
**Attendees:** Cassie Lewis (ByDesign), Autumn Fowers (Wayroo), Edmond Kim (Color Street), Brick B (Color Street)

---

## Summary

The demo went very well. Color Street is a heavy cash-and-carry company (~70% of stylists do cash and carry, many ex-LuLaRoe). They're 2 months post-migration from Exigo to a custom commission engine with Shopify as the storefront. Wayroo's core features resonated strongly, but there is one hard requirement before closing: **Shopify integration**.

---

## Features That Stood Out (by enthusiasm level)

### 🔴 Critical Requirement: Shopify Integration
Edmond Kim was unambiguous:
> "I am veering away from an isolated app. It's got to be within the lens of it being integrated into Shopify."
> "If you're going to come back with follow-up, it's got to be within the lens of it being integrated into Shopify."

They moved away from Exigo 2 months ago. Their reps use Shopify VO dashboards as their single back office. They want Wayroo to appear as a **sub-section within the Shopify VO** — not a standalone app. Edmond envisions stylists clicking "My Stash" from their VO without leaving Shopify. This is a **blocker** — a follow-up technical call is needed to scope the Shopify API integration.

---

### ✅ POS / Cash Register at Vendor Events
Strong positive reaction. Color Street is heavily event-driven and cash-and-carry. Brick estimated 70%+ of stylists carry personal inventory. Features that landed:
- Mobile POS replacing Square
- Barcode scanner support for fast checkout
- Replaces manual processes (Excel, Venmo/PayPal, riding around in cars)
- Shipping label printing for reps who ship to customers

---

### ✅ Inventory Management & Corporate Visibility
This was a dual win — both stylists AND corporate see value:

**For stylists:** Replace Excel spreadsheets. Wholesale orders auto-sync into the receiving screen instantly.

**For corporate:** Currently they have zero visibility into cash-and-carry inventory. Key use cases that excited them:
- See when a style "stocks out" corporately but stylists still have it (enable throwback targeting)
- Track product expiration (nail strips crack/dry out — customer service issue today)
- Clean up when brand changes or product is discontinued
- Know who has what when doing rep-to-rep transfers

---

### ✅ Customer Data / CRM Ownership
Strong pain point. Today, cash-and-carry sales happen on PayPal/Venmo with no invoice, no tracking, no customer record that Color Street owns. Edmond:
> "We don't own those customers."
> "If we integrate it to our site, then we have visibility into the customer that they're selling it to."

Wayroo solving this was a clear differentiator.

---

### ✅ Rep Personal Storefront ("My Stash")
Brick loved this concept and spontaneously rebranded it:
> "We could almost call it 'my stash'... the Color Street Stash. The app could be the stash."

They want it embedded within the Shopify VO (not a separate URL) but the concept of a personal e-commerce storefront for stylist inventory was well-received.

---

### ✅ Brand Control Over Resell
Cassie surfaced a pain point Color Street hadn't explicitly raised: reps currently post unprofessional photos, wrong descriptions, and sometimes renamed products. Wayroo pulling the official product catalog (professional images, descriptions, weights, SKUs) solves a brand integrity problem.

> "You guys would have better control over your brand and how it's being represented even on the resell." — Cassie (strong agreement from Brick)

---

### 🟡 Rep-to-Rep Wholesale Transfers
Brick confirmed this already happens informally (trading, selling surplus to each other). Formalizing it within Wayroo was interesting but not fully explored due to time.

---

### 🟡 Spendback Feature
Autumn mentioned it briefly as "can save you guys a bunch for wholesale purchasing" with "instant access to their funds." Edmond didn't have time to dig in but asked about it. **Should be a lead item in the follow-up call.**

---

### 🟡 Pricing Model
Asked at the very end. Key points that landed:
- Revenue model is primarily credit card transaction fees (not a heavy SaaS fee for corporate)
- White-labeling is optional — Brick said "function over fashion, like I mean, our ladies aren't like, you know... more if it was functional"
- Cost depends on active stylist count and customization level

---

## Key Differentiator That Landed Unexpectedly

**LuLaRoe origin story.** When Autumn mentioned Wayroo built the original LuLaRoe app, Brick immediately responded: "Oh, okay. That makes sense." This is highly relevant — Brick noted that many of Color Street's top stylists are **ex-LuLaRoe** who came over when LuLaRoe went through its changes in 2017. This is a credibility signal worth leading with.

---

## Blockers / Risks

| Risk | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Shopify integration (never done) | Hard requirement for Edmond. Need technical scoping call. Autumn believes API access makes it feasible. |
| SSO / user management | Edmond wants stylists to log into Shopify and just click into Wayroo — no separate login. |
| Payment processing separation | They want Wayroo transactions separate from Shopify Payments / Shop Pay. Autumn said Wayroo replaces Square — this needs to be clarified. |
