## 6. What You're Willing to Do

- **Run experiments and iterate fast** — test, measure, adjust. No attachment to tactics that aren't working.
- **Build GTM assets** — sales decks, one-pagers, case studies, battlecards, positioning documents. The output of strategy, not just the thinking.
- **Own the GTM strategy and product roadmap** — make the calls, set the priorities, align product development to market signals.
- **Use Claude as a team OS** — augment output rather than headcount. One person, founder-quality throughput.

---

## 7. What You're NOT Willing to Do

- **Be the content creator.** Writing and publishing LinkedIn posts, articles, and newsletters consistently is not your motion. If content is part of the GTM strategy, it needs a system or a person — not Sam doing it manually.
- **Run outreach yourself.** Daniel owns the sales motion. Sam owns the strategy and assets that make Daniel more effective. The boundary matters — crossing it doesn't scale.
- **Build for hypothetical requirements.** No features, frameworks, or assets built "just in case." Every deliverable has a specific milestone it unlocks.

---

## Key Insight

> **The GTM plan must be designed for a 20-hour-a-week strategist, not a full marketing team.**

Every tactic must be evaluated against this filter: *Can Sam build it, systemize it, and hand it to Daniel (or automate it with Claude) so it runs without constant intervention?*

The Paparazzi case study is the single highest-leverage starting point. Every other GTM asset — the positioning, the sales deck, the outreach sequences, the competitor battlecards — is more powerful with a published $31M proof point behind it. Build that first.

The winning formula is not more tactics. It is the right sequence: social proof → repeatable sales motion → documented playbook → scalable growth engine. Everything in the 90-day plan should point at that chain.
